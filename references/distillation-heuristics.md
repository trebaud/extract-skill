# Epistemic Distillation: Universal Principles

This document defines the heuristics for extracting high-density, actionable knowledge using principles of information science and philosophy.

## 1. Information Theory (Signal vs. Noise)

**Shannon Entropy**: Content that is predictable or common knowledge has zero informational value. Extract only the Surprisal—the unexpected or unique insights.

**Redundancy Elimination**: Treat repetitive examples as noise. Retain one representative archetype; discard the rest.

**Signal Maximization**: Every word must reduce uncertainty. If a sentence does not narrow the scope of action or understanding, it is noise.

## 2. Gricean Maxims (Linguistic Economy)

**Quantity**: Provide exactly as much information as required. No more, no less.

**Relation**: Only extract content that serves the Telos (the specific goal of the skill).

**Manner**: Avoid prolixity. Use precise nomenclature to replace verbose descriptions (e.g., replace "the way things are organized" with "Taxonomy").

## 3. Epistemic Filters (Truth & Utility)

**Occam's Razor**: Prefer the simplest explanation. Strip decorative complexity to find the most robust logic.

**Pareto Principle (80/20)**: 80% of value resides in 20% of the text. Identify the "vital few" principles that drive the "useful many" outcomes.

**First Principles**: Deconstruct content into its fundamental, irreducible truths. Discard industry jargon or transient "best practices" in favor of underlying logic.

## 4. Semiotic Abstraction

**Ladder of Abstraction**: Move from the concrete (specific examples) to the abstract (universal patterns). A pattern that applies to multiple domains is higher value than a single-use instruction.

**Structural Isomorphism**: Identify recurring structures. If two different processes share the same logical flow, extract the flow as a reusable template.

## 5. Teleological Validation (The Purpose Filter)

**Actionability**: If information cannot be used to make a decision or perform an action, it is "dead knowledge." Prioritize operative over declarative sentences.

**Future Utility**: Evaluate information based on its "half-life." Prioritize timeless principles over time-bound data.

## Summary of Extraction Workflow

1. **Filter Noise**: Strip marketing, anecdotes, and meta-talk (Shannon).
2. **Identify Signal**: Locate the core "How" and "Why" (DIKW).
3. **Abstract**: Convert specific instances into universal patterns (Semiotics).
4. **Compress**: Use the most precise language possible (Grice).
5. **Verify**: Ensure every extracted line justifies its token cost (Pareto).
